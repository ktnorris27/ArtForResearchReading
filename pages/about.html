Art For Research Reading (formerly Young Art Reading) is brand new art competition and exhibition for children and young people in Reading and the surrounding areas.

Art For Research Reading is a local community fundraising event, which will bring together schools, families, local organisations and community groups, to showcase the creativity of Reading’s children and young people while raising money for Cancer Research UK (Registered Charity No. 089464). For children and young people the competition is a great way to demonstrate their creative talents, offering an exciting opportunity to have their work judged by experts and exhibited in public while raising money for Cancer Research UK's Children & Young People campaign, which aims to accelerate progress, find new cures and kinder treatments for children’s cancers.

Each year the Art For Research Reading Committee will choose a theme for the competition and work with schools in Reading and the surrounding areas to encourage children and young people (aged between 4 and 14 years) to create and submit a picture inspired by this theme. For 2020/21, the theme for the brand new competition will be ‘The Environment’. To participate, students donate £2 to Cancer Research UK. Volunteers work with schools to launch the competition and collect students’ artwork for judging. An expert panel of judges then review the artwork, choosing entries from each participating school to exhibit and selecting an overall winner for each age group. The exhibition will take place at the University of Reading, across-two days, with a private opening and prize presentation for families, friends, school representatives and event sponsors and opportunities for members of the public, schools and local community groups to view the exhibition. The exhibition raises money for Cancer Research UK in a variety of ways, including donations for: exhibition programmes; tote bags, postcards and canvasses of children’s artwork; and the mounted and/or framed picture (reserved for families of competition entrants only).
